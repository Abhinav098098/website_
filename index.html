Solar Eclipses

Total Solar Eclipse

A total solar eclipse occurs when the Moon completely covers the Sun, as seen from Earth. This phenomenon can be observed in specific regions, providing a breathtaking view of the solar corona.

Partial Solar Eclipse

In a partial solar eclipse, only a portion of the Sun is obscured by the Moon. This results in a celestial event where part of the Sun is still visible, creating a crescent-like shape.

Annular Solar Eclipse

An annular solar eclipse occurs when the Moon covers the center of the Sun, leaving the outer edges visible. This creates a "ring of fire" effect, as the Sun's outer rim remains uncovered.
